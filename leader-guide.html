Manager Guide · Week 1

Your job is to build how they think

Not what they know. Not their product recall. The goal of Week 1 is a rep who sees hospital systems as systems — and asks questions from there.

The Only Thing That Matters This Week
By Day 5, your rep should be able to explain why hospitals struggle with glycemic control for 5 minutes — without mentioning GlytecOne, the platform, or anything related to a demo. That's the bar. Everything this week is calibrated toward it.
Day 1 — Welcome Conversation & Week Overview
Your Mindset Going In
Day 1 is not orientation. It's activation. By end of day, your new rep will have learned the GlytecOne story, passed the Readiness Check, and sent a real outreach email to a real prospect. That's Pat's philosophy — and it sets the tone for the whole week. Your job is to make sure the environment is ready, the expectations are set, and they feel genuinely set up to succeed — not overwhelmed.
Day
01

Morning Welcome — 30–45 Minutes

Do this before they open a laptop. Sit down, no screens. This conversation sets the frame for the entire week.
The Conversation — In This Order
Open With This
"Before we get into anything — I want to tell you how we think about Week 1 here."
Let them settle. This moment signals what kind of manager you are before they see anything in the LMS.
1 — The Philosophy (5 min)
Explain Pat's Day 1 framework: Learn the Story, Pass the Check, Send the Email.
"By end of today you'll have done all three. That sounds aggressive — it's actually the most respectful thing we can do, because it means we believe you're ready. We don't ease people in. We activate them."
2 — The Platform in Plain Language (5 min)
Give them the 60-second version of GlytecOne — in your own words. Not a pitch. Just the frame so the LMS lands better.
"GlytecOne is a clinical glycemic management platform. Two pillars: Care Enablement — that's the FDA-cleared algorithm-based dosing guidance that lives inside the EHR. And Performance Insights — system-wide analytics for quality teams and CMS readiness. We're not selling a tool. We're selling the ability to standardize something hospitals have never been able to standardize consistently."
3 — Why This Problem Matters (5 min)
Tell them why you personally believe in this category. A conviction statement — not a sales pitch.
If you have a moment from the field — a customer conversation, a case outcome, a stat that stopped you — share it now. This is the fastest way to build belief before the LMS does. Be specific. Generic enthusiasm doesn't land.
4 — The Week 1 Arc (5 min)
Walk them through the week — not the schedule, the arc.
"Day 1 is the story. Days 2–4 are earning the right to tell it — through evidence, compliance, and real account research. Day 5 is a signal moment: you'll explain to me why hospitals struggle with glycemic management without mentioning GlytecOne once. That's the bar. It sounds hard. You'll be ready."
5 — Daily Outreach Philosophy (5 min)
Explain why they send outreach every single day this week — and what it actually means.
"You're going to send at least one email every day this week. Not because we need the volume. Because the habit of reaching out is built in Week 1 or it isn't built at all. You won't have a perfect message today — that's fine. You'll have better ones by Friday, and you'll understand why."
6 — What You'll Get From Me (3 min)
Be specific about your role this week so there are no surprises.
"Day 3 I'll check in for 15 minutes — that's a thinking check, not a quiz. Day 5 you'll do a short pitch for me. Starting Week 2, we'll do discovery roleplays 2–3 times. My job is not to give you better answers. It's to help you ask better questions."
7 — Open Question (5 min)
"What do you want to be true by Friday that isn't true right now?"
This is the best question you can ask a new rep on Day 1. It tells you how they think about development, what they're anxious about, and what they're ambitious about. Listen without solving — and remember what they say. Reference it on Day 5.

Day
01

End of Day — Before the Email Goes Out

Read the email before it sends. Don't rewrite it — just clear it.
Three Questions Before You Clear It
1. Is the language SOP-34 compliant? (No "AI," no "real-time alerts," no outcome promises)

2. Is it a discovery opener — or a product pitch in disguise?

3. Does it sound like a human wrote it to a specific person — or like a template?
If it reads like a pitch
Don't rewrite it for them. Say: "What's the one thing this person cares about that you don't mention here?" Let them find the gap. Then let them fix it. That's 10x more valuable than you rewriting the email.

Day 3 — Know the Rules
Day
03

Your role today: 15-minute check-in + call shadow

Three days in. You're not assessing product knowledge. You're assessing whether they're thinking in systems.
15-Minute Check-In — Run It Like This
Open With
"What's actually breaking in the hospitals we've covered so far?"
Then Ask
"What patterns are you seeing across the cases?"
You're listening for: workflow, variability, behavior gaps. If they name systems dynamics, that's good. If they jump to product, redirect.
The Redirect (Use Anytime)
"What's the problem behind that?" / "Where does it actually break?" / "How consistent is that across teams?"
What Strong Thinking Looks Like
StrongWeak — redirect immediately
"Workflow breaks here""They need better tools"
"There's variation between providers""We could help by showing them..."
"Behavior doesn't follow the process""The platform addresses this by..."
"The system isn't enforced consistently""They should standardize"
Call Shadow — Your Setup
  • Live call preferred. Fathom recording works if nothing is available.
  • Brief them beforehand: "Watch for when the rep goes deeper. Note one question you wouldn't have thought to ask."
  • 5-minute debrief after: "What did you notice?" Let them observe first.

Day 5 — Signal Day
Day
05

Your role today: the 20-minute assessment

This is the Week 1 signal moment. Assess discovery thinking — not product knowledge.
The Prompt
Say This Exactly
"Explain why hospitals struggle with glycemic control — without mentioning Glytec, the platform, or anything related to a demo."
5 minutes. Timer running. Don't interrupt. Listen.
What You're Listening For
Strong answerWeak answer — note it, address in Week 2
"Execution isn't consistent across teams"Mentions platform, product, or features
"Workflow breaks under pressure""We could show them..." or "The demo would..."
"Variation between providers nobody can see""They need better technology"
"Behavior doesn't follow the process"Jumps to solution before completing the problem frame
After the Pitch — Three Questions Only
1. "What did you learn about the system this week?"
2. "Where do you think hospitals are most vulnerable — and why?"
3. "What didn't you ask in your discovery practice that you should have?"
Do not do this
Give them "better questions." Turn the debrief into a teaching session. Let them jump to GlytecOne. That's Week 2 territory.

Week 2 — Discovery Roleplays
Week
02

10–15 min roleplay + 10 min debrief, 2–3x this week

State the rule up front every time: "This is discovery only. If you go into solution mode, I'll stop you."
The Four Scenarios
Scenario 1 — "Everything Looks Fine"
You say: "We have protocols in place and strong outcomes."
Listen for: "How consistent is execution?" / "Where do delays occur?" / "How does it vary across teams?"
Scenario 2 — "We Tried Something Before"
You say: "We implemented something similar — it didn't stick."
Listen for: "Where did adoption break down?" / "What made it hard to sustain?" / "How was success defined?"
Scenario 3 — "No Problem"
You say: "We're not seeing major issues right now."
Listen for: "How do you measure consistency?" / "What happens under pressure?" / "Where does variation show up?"
Scenario 4 — "Misalignment"
You say: Nursing is on board. Physicians aren't.
Listen for: "How are decisions aligned across teams?" / "Where do perspectives differ?" / "Who actually drives change here?"
Debrief Questions — Ask Only These Three
1. "What did you learn about the system?"
2. "Where do you think it's breaking?"
3. "What didn't you ask — but should have?"
If they go into solution mode
"Pause — pretend we don't exist. What's actually happening in the hospital?"

Then: "What would need to be true for any solution to matter here?"
Common Pitfalls
You see thisYou do this
"What are you using today?"Redirect to workflow: "Before tools — what's the actual process?"
Early pitchingStop and reset immediately. Don't let it slide.
Surface-level answersAsk "What actually happens in practice?" every time.
Memorizing the casesAsk for the pattern across cases, not the facts from one.
Your Job — What It Is and Isn't
You're not here to teach the product. You're not here to fix their answers. You're not here to give them scripts.

You're here to help them see how systems actually work — and stay in discovery long enough to find out what's really broken. That's it.
