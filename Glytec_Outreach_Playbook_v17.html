Field Operating Layer

Objections are signals.

Every objection maps to one of the 3 Whys not yet established. The job is to diagnose which one, then ask a question that moves the conversation forward. Acknowledge. Reframe with observation. Ask one question. Advance.

Signal map
WHY CHANGE MISSING
"We're standardized already"
"No issues currently"
"Systems are in place"
WHY NOW MISSING
"Not a priority right now"
"We're focused elsewhere"
"No urgency here"
WHY GLYTEC MISSING
"We already have X"
"We rely on our EHR"
"We have processes"
BOLD ASK NOT EARNED
"Just send me something"
"Not sure it's worth a meeting"
"We're not evaluating anything"
What to listen for
Primary signal
Buyer admits variability, inconsistency, or lack of visibility.

"It depends on the unit." / "We don't always see it." / "Varies by provider."

This is a Why Change signal. Stay diagnostic.
Secondary signal
Buyer acknowledges exposure, concern, or accountability.

"Hard to explain." / "We'd need better visibility." / "That could be an issue."

This is a Why Now signal. Go one layer deeper.
Ownership signal
Buyer connects to leadership, risk, or internal decision-making.

"That would involve the CFO." / "We'd need alignment." / "That's something we should look at."

Bold Ask is now earned.
If you're explaining, you're losing. If they're reflecting, you're winning.
Persona objections
Bold Ask readiness check

Don't ask for the meeting until you've heard at least one Primary Signal, ideally a Secondary Signal. Without them, the ask lands as a pitch.

WEAK ASK
"Would you be open to a demo?"
STRONG ASK
"Most teams only get clarity on this once they compare how others are approaching the same challenge. If helpful, we can walk through that based on what you're seeing. Working session, no demo."
Manager coaching, what to inspect
Don't ask this
"How did the call go?"
"Are they interested?"
"When are they buying?"
Ask this instead
"What signal did you hear, exact words?"
"Primary, secondary, or ownership?"
"What did you do with it?"
"Did the signal deepen or stall?"
